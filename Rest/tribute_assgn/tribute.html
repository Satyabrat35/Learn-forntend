Bob Paisley

Here's a time line of Bob Paisley's life:

  • 1919: Born in a small village near Sunderland
  • 1933: Left school and started working at the coal mine
  • 1937: Signed for Bishop Auckland
  • 1939: After winning the Northern League championship, the FA Amateur Cup and the Durham County Challenge Cup, he signed for Liverpool FC
  • 1939: In in October he was called up by the Army who assigned him to the Royal Artillery in which he was a gunner in the 73rd Medium Regiment
  • 1940: Paisley was given clearance by the Army to play for Liverpool against Everton in the 1940 Liverpool Senior Cup final.But lost to Everton by 4-1
  • 1941: He was stationed in Egypt
  • 1943: Paisley went with the Eighth Army into Sicily and then into Italy
  • 1945: Paisley finally returned to England in 1945 and was stationed at Woolwich Arsenal until he was demobbed. Shortly before that, he met his future wife Jessie, a schoolteacher, on a train at Maghull
  • 1946: Bob and Jessie were married in Liverpool at All Souls Church, Springwood
  • 1946: Paisley eventually made his official debut on 5 January 1946 in Liverpool's first post-war competitive match
  • 1948: Scored his first goal in a League game against Wolverhampton Wanderers
  • 1954: After retiring in 1954, Paisley joined the Liverpool back room staff as a self-taught physiotherapist
  • 1959: The arrival of Bill Shankly as manager in December 1959 transformed the fortunes of the club
  • 1974: Following victory in the 1974 FA Cup Final, Shankly surprisingly announced his retirement and the Liverpool directors appointed Paisley as his replacement in the hope of maintaining continuity
  • 1976: Liverpool FC won the league and UEFA Cup
  • 1977: Team won the UEFA Cup
  • 1978: Team won the League title
  • 1979-82: Team went on to win the League title 4 times , 3 League Cups and one UEFA Cup.He became the first person to manage three European Cup-winning sides
  • 1983: Announced his retirement after winning the League
  • 1996: He died on 14 February 1996 at the age of 77 and was honoured by the club with the opening of the Paisley Gates at one of the entrances to Anfield
